Camera Prep

  • Charge your camera so it is ready when you need it.
  • Clear space on your memory card or device.
  • Bring extra batteries so you do not run out of power.
Page 2

Phone Details

  • Use the back camera for better image quality.
  • Disable flash unless you truly need it.
  • Remove filters so your image looks natural.
  • Disable digital zoom and move closer instead when possible.
Page 3

Final Check

  • Clean your lens before you start shooting.

Get the Shot

This section is all about creating stronger photos with intention, creativity, and focus.

Goal of the Photo Angles Light & Composition Focus on Subject
Page 1

Figure Out the Goal of the Photo

  • Ask yourself what you want the picture to show.
  • Are you capturing a person, a moment, a place, or a feeling?
  • Knowing your goal helps you make better choices before you tap the shutter.
Page 2

Experiment with Angles

  • Try shooting from above, below, or from the side.
  • Move around instead of taking every photo from eye level.
  • Small changes in angle can make a photo much more interesting.
Page 3

Use Light and Composition

  • Pay attention to where the light is coming from.
  • Try to keep your frame simple and intentional.
  • Move your subject or yourself to improve the overall look of the image.
Page 4

Focus on the Subject and Avoid Distractions

  • Make it clear what the viewer should look at first.
  • Check the background for clutter or distracting objects.
  • Keep the subject as the star of the photo.

Technical Tips

These are simple techniques that can make your photos look more polished without making things complicated.

Lighting Composition Stability Final Reminder
Page 1

Lighting

  • Good light can make almost any photo better.
  • Natural light is often the easiest and most flattering choice.
  • Avoid harsh overhead light when possible.
  • Try turning your subject toward the light for a clearer, brighter image.
Page 2

Composition

  • Think about how the elements are arranged in the frame.
  • Leave out anything that does not help the photo.
  • Use lines, spacing, and balance to guide the viewer’s eye.
Page 3

Stability

  • Hold your camera or phone as steady as possible.
  • Use both hands when you can.
  • Lean against a wall or rest your device on something stable if needed.
  • Less movement usually means a sharper picture.
Final Page

Most Important of All

Go take lots of pictures and have fun.

The best way to improve is to keep shooting, keep experimenting, and enjoy the process.
